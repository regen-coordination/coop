# Coop Vision

This document is about the destination, not the immediate hackathon cut and not the near-term
execution plan.

## 1. One-Line Vision

Coop becomes the operating system for community-owned coordination: a way for groups to capture
what they know, turn it into shared sensemaking and opportunity, preserve it as durable memory, and
act on it without surrendering privacy, agency, or governance to a centralized platform.

## 2. The Problem Behind The Product

Most communities already produce valuable context:

- open tabs
- shared links
- meeting notes
- field observations
- grant leads
- governance discussions
- incomplete drafts

The failure is not content creation. The failure is conversion.

Useful context does not reliably become:

- shared memory
- coordinated interpretation
- legible opportunity
- funding readiness
- durable evidence
- accountable next steps

Coop exists to close that conversion gap.

## 3. What Coop Is Trying To Become

Coop is not just a better capture tool.

The long-term product is a coordination substrate with four layers:

### 1. Capture layer

Communities gather signal where they already work: browser tabs, links, notes, media, and live
conversation.

### 2. Sensemaking layer

The system helps a group interpret signal locally before it becomes shared state. The point is not
to flood the group with generated text. The point is to turn fragments into legible opportunities,
funding leads, claims, evidence, and next steps.

### 3. Memory layer

What a community decides to keep becomes portable, attributable, and durable. Shared memory should
survive staff churn, tool churn, and hosting churn.

### 4. Action layer

Communities should be able to connect knowledge to bounded execution: publishing, delegation,
archive, capital formation, funding, governance, Green Goods work, and other forms of accountable
action.

## 4. Why This Matters

If communities cannot preserve and interpret their own knowledge, they cannot coordinate well.

That shows up as:

- repeated work
- missed opportunities
- weak funding continuity
- poor governance memory
- hidden power accumulation
- over-reliance on private heroic labor
- dependence on tools that treat community knowledge as extractable platform inventory

Coop aims at a different posture:

- local by default
- explicit when shared
- opportunity-aware when structured
- durable when kept
- bounded when acted on

## 5. Vision Anchors From The Durgadas Standards

We are using the
[Coordination Structural Integrity Suite](https://github.com/durgadasji/standards)
as an external guidance layer for the vision. The generative standards are especially relevant here.

### Sensemaking Standard

The vision is not "store more things." It is "help groups make sense together so they can see where
real opportunity, capital formation, and collective action are possible."

That means Coop should improve a community's ability to:

- notice disruption
- relate specific observations to larger patterns
- learn through action, not only analysis
- move on sufficient understanding instead of waiting for perfect certainty
- retain temporal continuity across meetings, campaigns, and decisions

### Four Batteries Capacity Standard

The product should strengthen community capacity across four dimensions:

- personal: people have the capacity to notice, contribute, and think clearly
- relational: people can surface truth without relational collapse
- contribution: the work feels meaningful and agentic
- mission: the group remembers why the work matters

If Coop increases information volume while draining these batteries, the product fails its own
mission.

### Conflict Transformation Standard

A real coordination system must eventually help communities stay coherent under disagreement.

That does not mean the current product needs a full conflict practice layer tomorrow. It means the
vision has to leave room for:

- visible disagreement as coordination data
- documented review and response paths
- durable memory around contested decisions
- organizational patterns that reduce invisible conflict debt

## 6. What Coop Is Not

The vision is weaker if we let it blur into adjacent categories.

Coop is not primarily:

- a generic note-taking app
- a social network
- an AI wrapper in search of a use case
- an autonomous trading or governance bot
- a token before it is a working product

Those frames all shrink the actual ambition.

## 7. Horizon View

### Horizon 1: Working loop

This is the current product center of gravity:

- capture
- local synthesis
- review
- publish
- archive
- bounded action

This horizon should already feel like "knowledge into opportunity," especially for funding leads,
grant work, and capital formation briefs.

### Horizon 2: Shared sensemaking surface

This is where the product becomes more obviously collective:

- richer graph exploration
- clearer opportunity and funding pathways
- stronger review rituals
- clearer archive and proof visibility
- cross-device continuity

### Horizon 3: Coop OS

This is where the primitives become reusable across different community forms:

- neighborhood groups
- research networks
- public-goods teams
- cooperative projects
- governance groups
- capital allocators and grant circles

In concrete terms, Coop OS should eventually be understandable as a community runtime made of:

- member devices running capture and review surfaces
- a shared coordination layer for policy, sync, memory, and proofs
- optional steward-operated hardware for always-on services such as local model caching, call
  ingestion, archive jobs, and coordination relays
- action integrations that can route structured opportunity into funding, staking, grants, or other
  bounded capital flows

### Horizon 4: Protocol-level public good

This is the longest horizon:

- durable coordination infrastructure
- portable shared memory
- accountable knowledge-to-action rails
- community ownership over the coordination substrate itself

For Greenpill Dev Guild, one concrete expression of this horizon is Coop acting as the knowledge,
evidence, and deliberation layer around a public-goods staking protocol: signal comes in, the coop
structures it, members review it, and capital allocation can happen against a more legible public
record.

## 8. Vision Outcomes

If the vision is successful, communities should be able to say:

- we lose less important context
- we can see what matters sooner
- we can turn research into opportunity faster
- we can assemble better funding and capital-formation context
- our shared memory survives turnover
- our decisions are easier to trace and justify
- our coordination does not depend on trusting one central platform to remember everything for us

## 9. Relationship To Current Product Posture

The vision is larger than what is shipped today. That does not make it speculative fluff. It means
the current product should be read as the first credible membrane between scattered context and
community-owned coordination.

The immediate proof is the working loop.
The long-term claim is that this loop can become a durable civic and organizational primitive.
