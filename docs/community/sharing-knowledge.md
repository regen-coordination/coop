# Sharing Knowledge

Coop is not only about capture. The real product value shows up when a group decides what should
become shared knowledge and what should remain private working material.

## The Publish Boundary

The most important rule is simple:

- captured and drafted material can exist locally
- shared coop memory begins only when a member explicitly publishes

That boundary keeps the product understandable. Members can gather signal aggressively without
turning every rough note into group-visible state.

## What Can Be Shared

Once your Loose Chickens have been rounded up and refined, the system is designed around artifacts
such as:

- funding leads
- research summaries
- evidence and source bundles
- suggestions for next steps
- shared reflections that help the coop calibrate what matters

Each published artifact can be routed into one or more coops when that makes sense.

## Where Shared Knowledge Shows Up

After publish, knowledge becomes visible in the Coop Feed and in the board view of the shared graph.
Those surfaces make it easier to understand:

- who contributed context
- what draft or capture a shared artifact came from
- what should be reviewed next
- which items carry durable archive receipts

In the current extension, the normal movement is:

- `Popup` and `Chickens` for capture, working drafts, and publish prep
- `Coops` for the shared result after publish
- `Nest` for protected admin and operator follow-up
- `Roost` only when a linked Green Goods garden needs member work submission

## What Stays Local

A lot of useful material should stay local until someone makes a deliberate choice:

- raw captures that are not ready for the group
- drafts that still need editing
- receiver intake that has not been shaped yet
- low-signal material that is helpful for one person but not the whole coop

This is a feature, not a failure. Not every fragment deserves to become communal memory.

## Archive And Provenance

When a coop wants durable proof and long-memory, it can archive artifacts and snapshots. That creates
archive receipts on top of the shared artifact story, giving the group a stronger provenance path
than "someone remembers where we found this."

## Why This Matters

Communities do better when they can tell the difference between:

- private collection
- active review
- shared memory
- durable archive

They also do better when they can tell the difference between ordinary member work and protected
operator work. Green Goods follows that split: members submit work, while trusted operators handle
approvals, assessments, admin sync, and later Hypercert or Karma GAP packaging.
